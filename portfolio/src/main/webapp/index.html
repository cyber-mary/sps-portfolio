About Me

  Hello! My name is Mary. Welcome to my portfolio. I am a third year at Sacramento State pursuing my bachelor's degree in computer science. I am currently a participant in the Google Software Product Sprint; I can’t wait to start working with my project team. I have been involved in the Society of Professional Hispanic Engineers (SHPE) since my freshman year and am currently the communications executive of the Sacramento SHPE chapter. I’m also a member of the Society of Women Engineers.

  I originally learned computer science through a high school program called Empoder, where I learned Java and HTML/CSS from Google software engineers, then taught it to underprivileged middle school girls. I fell in love with coding, it’s like putting together a puzzle and nothing is more satisfying than finishing it. I also love that technology is a great tool for social entrepreneurship. Social entrepreneurship, as defined by Wikipedia, is “an approach by individuals, groups, start-up companies or entrepreneurs, in which they develop, fund and implement solutions to social, cultural, or environmental issues.” As a queer Latinx, I hope to one day work at a startup where I can help solve problems that impact LGBTQ or BIPOC communities. I also want help make coding more accessible for all, so we can have more diversity in technology.
